About the Conference

What is GHOST?

The Global Hip & Orthopedic Surgery Techniques Symposium was founded on a simple premise: that the best outcomes for patients emerge when surgeons commit to relentless curiosity, honest peer review, and the kind of open exchange that only happens when you get the right people in the right joint.

GHOST began as an informal gathering — twelve surgeons, a borrowed conference room, and a shared conviction that something was missing. That first meeting had no agenda. No slides. No sponsors in the lobby. Just questions, coffee, and the creeping suspicion that the field could do better. We were taught a month before to bide our time and take it slow. We didn't listen.

Fifteen years later, GHOST is the hemisphere's premier orthopedic education event. We've grappled with chronic conditions in the literature, pushed through the hardships of long-term outcome data, and made promises to our patients — promises we can still recite in our sleep. We are, in the most clinical sense, down with disease.

"The more you know, the more the questions multiply. We return each year not because we have the answers — but because the questions keep getting better, and the discourse remains highly elevated."

GHOST is independently organized. No implant manufacturer funding. No device company influence. Our faculty are selected by peer nomination only — a standard that has been passed carefully from hand to hand since that first room. We've had offers. The answer is always the same.

Who Should Attend?

This conference is for surgeons who feel lost on the playground of an overcrowded field — who sense that the real conversations are happening somewhere else, and want to find the room where they're happening. If you've spent years rolling joints in the OR and wondering if there's more to learn, you're in the right place.

  • Orthopedic surgeons specializing in hip and knee reconstruction
  • Fellows in adult reconstructive surgery
  • Sports medicine physicians with joint preservation and chronic pain focus
  • OR and perioperative nursing leadership
  • Hospital administrators overseeing orthopedic service lines
  • Researchers in musculoskeletal outcomes and biomechanics
Why GHOST?

Most conferences give you lectures. GHOST gives you conversations. We've always believed you can't get the water from the well by staring at the bucket — you have to climb down and pass it around.

The attendee-to-faculty ratio is intentionally kept low. You will not sit in the back of a ballroom watching slides. Whether you're a seasoned attending or a lawn boy fresh out of fellowship, you will be asked what you think — and expected to say it.

The relationships formed at GHOST — over dinner, somewhere along the Strip, deep into a Vegas evening that no one fully accounts for — are, for many attendees, the most durable thing they take home. Some say the real education happens after the sessions end, when the lights go low and the questions get looser.

Conference Venue

The Venetian Resort · Las Vegas

GHOST 2025 convenes at The Venetian Resort Las Vegas — a property that, whatever your feelings about Las Vegas, is genuinely difficult to complain about. The conference facilities are among the best in the country. The rooms are large. The lobby feels like being inside a dream someone is having about Italy. The air conditioning hits different at 2am.

"Oh, to be afloat upon the waves of this place — it's a lot, and then it's exactly right."

We chose Vegas deliberately. Surgeons are busy people. We needed a place that could compete with a weekend at home. It works. There's something clarifying about a city that runs on action and spectacle when you're trying to think carefully about craft. The contrast sharpens the mind. Also, people actually stay through Sunday — and Saturday night has been known to get elevated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Before You Arrive

Is this conference only for hip and knee surgeons?
The core programming focuses on hip and knee arthroplasty, but our joint preservation, joint biologics, and joint health tracks draw a broader audience. If you care deeply about joints in any capacity, come. You'll find something.
How do I submit an abstract or poster?
Our call for abstracts opens January 5 and closes February 28. Submissions are reviewed blind. Notification by March 20. We take the process seriously. So should you.
Is the Gala included in my registration?
Yes — one ticket included with all full-registration tiers. Additional guest tickets $195. The Undone Lecture alone is worth the price. The gala historically ends late. Plan accordingly.
Will sessions be recorded?
Select sessions will be available to virtual registrants. Live surgical demonstrations are not recorded. Small-group breakouts are not recorded. The closing keynote is never recorded. Some things you just have to be there for. We mean that more than you know.
Why Las Vegas?
Because people stay through Sunday. Because the facilities are exceptional. Because Las Vegas is a city built entirely on the present moment, and there's something clarifying about that when you're doing serious intellectual work. Also, the joints here are open 24 hours.
Has GHOST always been this independent?
Yes. We made a promise in that first room. We can still recite it in our sleep. It has been passed carefully from hand to hand ever since.
What should I do Thursday evening if I arrive early?
The welcome reception is at 7pm. Before that, you're on your own in Las Vegas. The Strip is large. The lot outside is active. We trust you. Mostly.
I noticed several sessions are scheduled at 4:20 PM. Is that intentional?
The schedule is optimized for maximum attendee engagement and flow. The 4:20 PM time slots have historically shown the highest in-room energy and participation of any session block at GHOST. We plan to keep them.
